TITLE: H1 — Kinetic-to-MHD Conversion Stability
PROBLEM: 14.1 MeV neutrons create random shockwaves in liquid Pb‑Li; chaotic momentum transfer induces turbulence, breaking smooth MHD rotation necessary for field generation.

OBJECTIVE: Demonstrate controlled, directional conversion of neutron kinetic energy into coherent tangential rotation without mechanical channels.

CONCEPTUAL APPROACHES:
- Directional Scattering Lattice (field-induced): test whether high-frequency EM fields can transiently align conduction pathways in a surrogate liquid to bias scattering angles.
- Viscoelastic Field Dampener: induce temporary, reversible increase in effective viscosity/elasticity under applied RF/EM fields to absorb local shocks.
- Micro-flow electromagnetic guides: use patterned, phased external EM drives to create large-scale shear flows without physical vanes.

IMMEDIATE EXPERIMENTS (bench):
1) Surrogate fluid tests: use Galinstan or saline MHD analog to evaluate field-alignment under strong oscillatory magnetic fields.
2) Pulse-impact tests: simulate directional kinetic impulse (laser shock or particle-beam analog) and measure post-impact flow coherence with/without field.
3) Microstructure detection: probe field-induced micro-ordering (X‑ray scattering or ultrafast optical probe) in conductive liquids.

SUCCESS CRITERIA (6 months):
- Measured increase in tangential momentum transfer fraction vs control (>10% target initial).
- Demonstrated damping of induced turbulence spikes by >50% with field conditioning.
- Reproducible field-on / field-off behavior and recovery.

RESOURCE NEEDS:
- Small MHD loop (liters), strong pulsed magnets, fast diagnostics (PDV, PIV), surrogate impulse source, materials team for Pb‑Li handling plan.
