TITLE: H2 — Survivable Window for EM Harvesting
PROBLEM: Metamaterial sensors must receive synchrotron/microwave output near plasma but are vulnerable to heat, charged particles and neutrons.

OBJECTIVE: Build a resilient, high-bandwidth EM sensor interface that survives intense pulsed exposure and delivers low-latency field-gradient data.

CONCEPTUAL APPROACHES:
- Self‑healing liquid curtain: thin, high‑velocity Li film that flows between plasma and metamaterial; actively magnetically shaped.
- Pulsed exposure + replaceable tile model: metamaterial tiles designed for short exposure windows then swapped or flushed.
- Remote optical coupling: use frequency-selective near‑field coupling or waveguide channels embedded in flowing liquid to isolate electronics.

IMMEDIATE EXPERIMENTS (bench):
1) Metamaterial tile tests under targeted microwave flux; measure conversion efficiency and thermal tolerance.
2) Test thin liquid-metal films (Li analog or low‑melting eutectic) under high-heat flux and magnetically shaped flow to measure erosion and transmissivity.
3) Build a mock-up "antenna + liquid-curtain" cell to quantify EM coupling vs particle/heat loading.

SUCCESS CRITERIA (6 months):
- Demonstrate >70% EM capture efficiency with a flowing liquid buffer under pulsed heat/particle loading analog.
- Show reconstitution/replacement procedure for damaged tiles within practical cycle times.
- Validate latency and SNR of sensor output adequate for state-compression input.

RESOURCE NEEDS:
- Microwave source, metamaterial fabrication access, liquid-metal handling capabilities, high-speed EM diagnostics.
