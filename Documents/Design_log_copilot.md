PROJECT: Nested Spherical Self‑Powered Fusion Reactor
PURPOSE: A conceptual, interdependent fusion reactor design that uses the reactor's own outputs to contain, stabilize, and harvest power without relying on steam turbines or massive external superconducting coils.

TIMELINE SUMMARY (conceptual steps taken)
- Began with fusion fundamentals (why energy is released; D-T chosen for accessibility and energy density).
- Established Fusion Triad: Heat, Density (pressure), Confinement.
- Rejected standard brute-force Earth replication of stellar gravity; focused on converting output energy into containment and power.
- Shifted from solid first-wall + static coils to a liquid‑layer, self-powered approach.
- Evolved geometry from toroidal/tokamak thinking to nested spherical design.
- Reversed initial anchor idea: moved to Plasma core surrounded by molten iron inner layer, Pb-Li outer liquid layer, then structural casing.
- Built interdependence loop: neutrons → fluid motion → magnetic field → harvestable electricity; synchrotron → metamaterial → control.
- Identified four moonshot engineering hurdles; proposed conceptual solutions for each.
- Added Temporal Stabilization and Hybrid Predictive-Adaptive control with precomputed Stability Map powered by a quantum processor concept.
