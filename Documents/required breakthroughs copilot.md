FOUNDATIONAL RISKS & REQUIRED BREAKTHROUGHS
- Field-induced micro-lattice in a bulk liquid metal: no proven experimental mechanism; requires discovery of "smart MHD fluids" or new EM/material coupling regimes.
- Liquid curtain reliability next to 100M°C plasma: maintaining laminar, continuously replaced film practically and magnetically is unproven at required fluxes.
- Quantum processor capability: needs orders-of-magnitude improvements in practical quantum coherence and problem-specific algorithms for real-time, low-latency operation.
- Layer boundary stability: preventing Rayleigh–Taylor and Kelvin–Helmholtz mixing between high-density molten layers with purely electromagnetic separation demands new field geometries and active reinforcement strategies.
- Inductive harvest coupling efficiency: high-power, close-coupled coil arrays must be designed so they do not perturb the containment fields they extract energy from.
- Materials survivability and activation: neutron flux will cause material damage and transmutation; alloys/metamaterials must survive or be rapidly repairable/replenishable.
- Thermodynamic realities: some energy will unavoidably thermalize; the design minimizes this but must meet realistic conversion and waste-heat management engineering limits.
