HURDLE 1: Kinetic-to-MHD Conversion Stability
- Problem: Random 14.1 MeV neutron impacts create violent, localized shockwaves (turbulence) that would break smooth rotational MHD flow.
- Conceptual Solutions:
  * Directional Scattering Lattice: use resonant magnetic field to induce temporary micro-orientation in Pb-Li, biasing momentum transfer into tangential direction.
  * Viscoelastic Field Dampener: field-induced temporary elasticity/viscous response that absorbs shocks and releases smooth rotational energy.
  * Micro-structured sacrificial inner sub-layer or microchannels (non-physical guides) implemented electromagnetically rather than mechanically.

HURDLE 2: The Survivable Window (Metamaterial Barrier)
- Problem: Metamaterial sensors/aperture need proximity to plasma EM output but would be destroyed by heat and neutrons.
- Conceptual Solutions:
  * Self-Healing Liquid Curtain: thin, fast lithium film between plasma and metamaterial; actively cooled and refreshed to absorb immediate damage.
  * Magnetic shaping of the curtain (LMMHD flow control) to keep it laminar and in position.
  * Consider metamaterial designs that tolerate short, pulsed exposure and rely on rapid replacement/repair.

HURDLE 3: Inductive Field Harvest Efficiency vs Stability
- Problem: Stable fields are low-dΦ/dt (low harvest); high harvest requires field dynamism which threatens stability.
- Conceptual Solutions:
  * Temporal Stabilization: run baseline control at very high frequency; embed small, controlled ω_pulse to maximize dΦ/dt intentionally.
  * Phased external bias coils to introduce and control the targeted oscillation without destabilizing the system.
  * Self-compensating inductive coil array that dynamically matches coupling for efficiency while minimizing interference.

HURDLE 4: Real-Time Control / Quantum Processor
- Problem: Modeling/predicting plasma boundary dynamics at nanosecond scales is computationally immense.
- Conceptual Solutions:
  * Hybrid approach: precomputed high-dimensional Stability Map from comprehensive quantum-assisted offline simulation; real-time system performs ultra-fast state compression and map lookup.
  * Quantum Processor specialized for boundary-layer MHD predictions, powered by harvested EM energy.
  * Massive, low-latency metamaterial sensor grid providing parallelized field-gradient data; state compression reduces raw input to actionable coordinates for the map lookup.
